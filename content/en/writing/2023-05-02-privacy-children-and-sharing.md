Social media brought entirely new ways of handling privacy — and while adults can more or less make informed choices about what they want, children are completely at the mercy of their parents. Except the price — in the form of lost privacy, humiliating photos in the public space, or "just" precisely targeted ads to make them buy things — is paid by the children, not the parents.

I tried to explain the issue in what was perhaps the first article about sharenting published in Czech media, on Heroine. [You can find the article here (in Czech)](https://www.heroine.cz/spolecnost/1036-komu-patri-soukromi-o-lajcich-ktere-budou-mrzet-vase-deti).

And because I also care about what we do within the party, in 2022 I proposed a successful member initiative that bans the use of children in Pirate party advertising. Small steps, but at least something.

(What individual Pirate politicians post on their own social media is, of course, their personal decision.)
