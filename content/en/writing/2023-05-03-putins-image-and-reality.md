In February 2022, our world changed dramatically for the worse. The Russian Federation escalated its aggression against Ukraine and attempted a three-day war that was supposed to end the existence of a sovereign Ukraine.

So far it looks different, but a crucial part of the conflict's image (and of previous conflicts) in the Czech space has been the image of Vladimir Putin. Through his aggressive policies in Russia and its distant neighbourhood, he was hailed as an effective — and highly foresighted — strategist, a man of calibre whom the West simply cannot match.

For Deník Referendum, I tried to write about why this is, more than anything else, a bad interpretation of two decades of his rule. And in retrospect, it seems I was rather on target.
