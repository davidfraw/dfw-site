Hey,

Starting Tuesday, I'll have about 15 days where I'll be completely off the grid. I'm going through ketamine-assisted therapy, to which I've added a bunch of other therapies and time to somehow think things through and prepare. One of the things my therapist really impressed upon me is that I should do nothing beyond the absolute bare minimum. Unfortunately, there was no other time to do it, because appointments are extremely rare.

It's admittedly very uncomfortable for me, because I haven't managed to fully switch off work for quite a long time, and I usually just replace it with other work. I'm also a lifelong teetotaller and that's important to me: that's why I resisted antidepressants for quite a long time and only gradually accepted them, that's why it's still incredibly hard for me to say "I'm going to take psychedelics," and it took a long time before I agreed to the suggestion. And now here we are and I'm a bit in shock that I'm 34 years old and about to be out of my mind for the first time. But I promise I'll tell you about it afterwards.

So partly for awareness (because if this were talked about more, maybe I would have gotten to it sooner), and because it might help someone, and largely to explain it to myself, I'm writing this piece.

I'm trying it because I don't have many other options left, and even though it will only be the beginning of another journey, I've walked the other paths and they mostly didn't work. Since 2017, I've been through the hands of various therapists and psychiatrists, tried meditation, exercise, and antidepressants of many types.

Sometimes I have periods where I really sleep badly, sometimes things go OK. Occasionally a very irrational — and all the more unpleasant — thanatophobia makes itself felt, which at least guarantees that I probably won't try to take my own life again.

I have a big problem with my relationship to myself, and above all — I live with treatment-resistant depression. I'm incredibly lucky that my version means that while I don't really feel much and don't feel like living, I'm fully capable of getting out of bed in the morning, working, and doing things. I've also learned a lot of workarounds. For instance, constantly new projects and obligations give me perspective; regular exercise doesn't exactly thrill me, but it gives things some order and I can tick off "done." And I can also work myself up to being funny, because when it brings joy to other people — hey, it's worth it.

My depression is like an annoying buzz at the back of my head, a constant undertone of "why?" — and even though I'm very, very convinced that there's a point to being here, it's really quite a drag to keep carrying this heavy backpack.

So I'm going to give those fourteen days to it now.

If you urgently need something, text me on Discord or my phone. I can't and don't want to completely shed all obligations — I'm working on a few beautiful things that need finishing up, maybe I promised you something — but overall I'll really try to postpone most of my commitments for those two weeks.

In the meantime, I'll be going outside, exercising, reading, taking terribly expensive drugs, and writing things for fun.
